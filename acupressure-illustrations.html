Acupressure Point Illustrations

Hand-drawn anatomical guides with exact point locations

Each illustration shows the precise location of an acupressure point based on the WHO Standard Acupuncture Point Locations (Western Pacific, 2008). Press firmly with your thumb or middle finger and hold for the time indicated. Breathe slowly throughout.

KIDNEY MERIDIAN — KD1
Gushing Spring
涌泉 — Yǒng Quán
Where
Sole of the foot — curl your toes and a small depression appears. The point is about one-third of the way down from the base of the toes, in the hollow between the 2nd and 3rd toe bones (metatarsals).
How to press
Because the skin on the sole is thick, you need firm, deep pressure. Use your thumb to burrow into the depression. When your thumb tires, use your middle knuckle (fold your hand into a loose fist). For a stronger effect: place a golf ball on the floor, put the upper-third hollow of your sole on top of it, and slowly lean in — hold for 1 minute while breathing slowly. A lacrosse ball works well too.
Time
1–3 minutes on each foot
Effect
Draws heat down from the head and face. Grounds rising Yang energy. The most powerful cooling and anchoring point in the body — especially useful during a hot flash or when feeling flushed and agitated. Also calms racing thoughts and anxiety.
KIDNEY MERIDIAN — KD3
Great Ravine
太溪 — Tài Xī
Where
Inner ankle — in the soft hollow between the peak of the inner ankle bone (medial malleolus) and the Achilles tendon, exactly at the level of the ankle bone tip. When you press, your finger should sink into a soft "ravine" — you are not pressing the bone or the tendon itself, but the fleshy space between them.
How to press
Press firmly with your thumb into the hollow — you should feel a dull ache or fullness, which confirms the right location. For a more powerful effect, use the pinch technique: place your thumb on KD3 (inside of ankle) and your index finger on the outside of the ankle (BL60), pinching both sides of the Achilles tendon at the same time. Steady pressure or slow circular massage.
Time
1–2 minutes on each side
Effect
Tonifies Kidney Yang — the root of all body energy. Helps with night sweats, internal heat, fatigue, and hormonal imbalance. The source point of the Kidney meridian.
LARGE INTESTINE MERIDIAN — LI4
Union Valley
合谷 — Hé Gǔ
Where
Back of the hand, in the webbed area between thumb and index finger. Two ways to find the exact spot: (1) Muscle bulge method: squeeze thumb and index together — a small muscle bulges up in the web; LI4 is at the highest point of that bulge. (2) Bone method: feel the long bone of your index finger and find its midpoint on the thumb side.
How to press
Use a pincer grip — place your pressing thumb on top of the hand (on the LI4 spot) and your index finger on the palm side directly underneath. Aim the pressure toward the bone of your index finger — you are compressing the muscle against the bone, not just squeezing skin. Apply firm, steady pressure or small circular rubs for 1–2 minutes. You should feel a distinct dull ache or a "heavy" sensation spreading through the hand.
Time
1–2 minutes on each hand
Effect
Regulates sweating and body temperature. Moves Qi throughout the body, relieves tension and headaches. One of the most powerful general-purpose points in acupuncture.
⚠ Do not use during pregnancy
SPLEEN MERIDIAN — SP6
Three Yin Intersection
三陰交 — Sān Yīn Jiāo
Where
Inner leg — find the highest peak of your inner ankle bone and place four finger-widths above it. The point is just behind the shinbone (tibia), not on the bone itself. Slide your fingers off the flat surface of the shin toward the back of the leg until they drop into the soft fleshy area — there is often a slight hollow there. The spot is typically tender when pressed correctly.
How to press
Press into the soft tissue behind the edge of the shinbone — never directly onto the bone. Use your thumb or index finger with firm, steady pressure or circular massage. The correct spot feels noticeably more tender than surrounding tissue.
Time
1–3 minutes on each side
Effect
The meeting point of three Yin meridians — Kidney, Liver, Spleen. Supports hormones, reduces hot flashes, improves sleep, and calms the mind. One of the most used points in women's health.
⚠ Do not use during pregnancy
