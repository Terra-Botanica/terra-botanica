Acupressure Point Illustrations

Hand-drawn anatomical guides with exact point locations

Each illustration shows the precise location of an acupressure point based on the WHO Standard Acupuncture Point Locations (Western Pacific, 2008). Press firmly with your thumb or middle finger and hold for the time indicated. Breathe slowly throughout.

KIDNEY MERIDIAN — KD1
Gushing Spring
涌泉 — Yǒng Quán
Where
Sole of the foot — in the deepest depression when the toes are flexed, at the junction of the anterior 1/3 and posterior 2/3 of the line from the heel to the web margin between the 2nd and 3rd toes.
How to press
Press firmly with your thumb or massage in small slow circles. You can also use your knuckle. Rub vigorously to generate warmth during a hot flash.
Time
1–2 minutes on each foot
Effect
Draws heat down from the head and face. Grounds rising Yang energy. The most powerful cooling and anchoring point in the body — especially useful during a hot flash or when feeling flushed and agitated.
KIDNEY MERIDIAN — KD3
Great Ravine
太溪 — Tài Xī
Where
Inner ankle — in the soft hollow between the tip of the medial ankle bone (medial malleolus) and the Achilles tendon, at the level of the ankle bone tip.
How to press
Press firmly with your thumb into the hollow. You should feel a slight ache or fullness — that confirms the correct location. Gentle circular massage is also effective.
Time
1–2 minutes on each side
Effect
Tonifies Kidney Yang — the root of all body energy. Helps with night sweats, internal heat, fatigue, and hormonal imbalance. The source point of the Kidney meridian.
LARGE INTESTINE MERIDIAN — LI4
Union Valley
合谷 — Hé Gǔ
Where
Back of the hand — at the highest point of the muscle mound between the thumb and index finger when they are pressed together. On the line of the 2nd metacarpal bone, midpoint.
How to press
Press firmly with the opposite thumb, squeezing between your thumb (below) and index finger (above). Press into the mound. You should feel a strong, spreading ache.
Time
About 1 minute on each hand
Effect
Regulates sweating and body temperature. Moves Qi throughout the body, relieves tension and headaches. One of the most powerful general-purpose points in acupuncture.
⚠ Do not use during pregnancy
SPLEEN MERIDIAN — SP6
Three Yin Intersection
三陰交 — Sān Yīn Jiāo
Where
Inner leg — 4 finger-widths above the tip of the medial ankle bone (your own four fingers placed above the ankle, touching bone side), just posterior (behind) the tibia shin bone.
How to press
Place four fingers above the ankle bone to find the level, then press into the soft area just behind the shin bone at that height. Firm, sustained pressure or slow circular massage.
Time
1–2 minutes on each side
Effect
The meeting point of three Yin meridians — Kidney, Liver, Spleen. Supports hormones, reduces hot flashes, improves sleep, and calms the mind. One of the most used points in women's health.
⚠ Do not use during pregnancy
